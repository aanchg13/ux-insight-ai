Interviewer: Can you walk me through how you book a doctor's appointment?

User: It's a nightmare honestly. I usually have to call because the online system never works properly on my phone. I've tried three times and it always crashes before I can confirm.

Interviewer: What happens when you call instead?

User: I'm on hold for like 20 minutes minimum. And then half the time they tell me the doctor I want isn't available for six weeks. Nobody tells you that online. You find out after waiting forever.

Interviewer: How do you keep track of your appointments once booked?

User: They send a letter. An actual paper letter. In 2024. By the time it arrives I've already forgotten what time it was. I write it on a sticky note on my fridge which is not ideal.

Interviewer: What would your ideal booking experience look like?

User: Just let me see real availability online, book in two clicks, and send me a text reminder the day before. That's it. Nothing fancy. I just want to feel like my time is respected.

Interviewer: Have you ever missed an appointment because of these issues?

User: Twice. And they charged me a cancellation fee both times even though I never got a proper reminder. That really put me off the whole system.
